Memory Lane

PlayStation 4

The PlayStation 4 is a home video game console developed by Sony. It's the 8th generation console, succedding it's predecessor being the PlayStation 3 [PS3].

Xbox One

The Xbox One is a home video game console developed by Microsoft. It's the sucessor to the Xbox 360 and the third generation of the Xbox franchise.

PlayStation 3

The PlayStation 3 is a home video game console developed by Sony. This console is the successor the PlayStation 2 and is the next generation of the PlayStation brand of consoles.

Xbox 360

The Xbox 360 is a home video game console developed by Microsoft. As the soccor to the origianl Xbox, this became the second developed console and it expanded on the xbox live expierence.

PlayStation 2

The PlayStation 2 is a home video game console developed by Sony. This was the first successor to the original PlayStation which was competing with the likes of Nintendo GameCube, Sega's Dreamcast and Microsoft's Xbox console.

Xbox

The Xbox is the first home video game console developed by Microsoft. This became the first installemnt of the Xbox console franchise and would be the first console to start social features by playing online anywhere in the world.

Nintendo Wii

The Nintendo Wii is a home video game console developed by Nintendo. This is a first console to use real motion caption to enhance gameplay and online play.

Nintendo GameCube

The Nintendo 64 is a home video game console developed by Nintendo. This console was known for it's renoun graphicsw for it's time and would mark the birth of the console wars between Sony's PS2, Sega's Dreamcast, and Microsoft's Xbox.

Nintendo 64

The Nintendo 64 is a home video game console developed by Nintendo. This was the last major console to use ROM cartridges as storage and CD's. This console competed with the likes of the PS1



What is Gaming

Gaming refers to playing electronic games, whether through consoles, computers, mobile phones or another medium altogether. Gaming is a nuanced term that suggests regular gameplay, possibly as a hobby. Although traditionally a solitary form of relaxation, online multiplayer video games have made gaming a popular group activity as well.
